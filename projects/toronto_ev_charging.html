Toronto EV Charging Accessibility Map

Project Summary

As Toronto accelerates toward its Net-Zero by 2040 goals, access to public EV charging remains highly uneven across the city — particularly in low-income, high-density, and transit-oriented communities. This solo project, developed for the 2025 Esri Canada App Challenge, investigates spatial disparities in EV charging availability using a multi-criteria demand index across 585 census tracts.

Through a dual-interface deployment — a public-facing StoryMap and a planner-focused Experience Builder App — this project supports equitable transportation planning by revealing where policy, infrastructure, and socioeconomic stressors collide. The final map enables decision-makers to explore high-demand, underserved zones and download tract-level data for planning and grant support.

Key Insights

  • 60%+ of high-demand zones are concentrated in apartment-dense neighborhoods where residents lack access to at-home chargers, which posing major equity concerns.
  • 30+ GO Train stations across Toronto are located in charging deserts, lacking public EV infrastructure within 500 meters, despite high commuter traffic.
  • Areas with both high EV ownership and low charger access (e.g., Scarborough, North York) highlight critical mismatches in deployment strategy.
  • Transit-adjacent employment zones where long-distance commuters or rideshare drivers park. This shows demand for workplace charging that is not currently met.
  • The tool enables layered filtering of EV demand factors (housing, income, proximity) to help cities prioritize investment in the most infrastructure-poor neighborhoods.

Methods

  • EV Demand Score: Developed a composite index using 6 weighted variables:
    • Proximity to underserved GO Train stations (+3 points)
    • Absence of public EV chargers (+3 points)
    • High percentage of low-income residents (LIM-AT) (+2 points if >20%)
    • High apartment density (5+ stories, where home charging is unavailable) (up to +4 points)
    • High EV ownership levels (up to +4 points)
    • Employment hubs (job centers requiring workplace chargers) (+2 points)
    Assigned a final EV Demand Score to each census tract, classifying areas into low (0-4), medium (5-7), and high (8+) demand zones.
  • Proximity Analysis: 500m buffer around GO Train stations to detect charger gaps.
  • Spatial Join: Allocated FSA-level EV ownership and employment zone data to census tracts.
  • Layer Classification: Natural Breaks (Jenks) and Quantile for thematic visualization.
  • UX Design: Experience Builder allows layer toggling, filtering, and data export (GeoJSON/CSV).
